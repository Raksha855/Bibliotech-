Lok Sabha – House of the People

“Lok Sabha is not just the lower house, it is the beating heart of Indian democracy.” — Bibliotech

Introduction

Lok Sabha is the directly elected body of the Indian Parliament. It represents the will of the people and plays a central role in law-making and executive accountability.

Composition

  • Maximum Strength: 552 (530 from states, 20 from UTs, 2 nominated – now removed)
  • Current Strength: 545 members
  • Tenure: 5 years (Article 83), can be dissolved earlier by the President
  • Quorum: Minimum 1/10th members required for proceedings

Election

  • Members are elected through **Direct elections** by **universal adult suffrage** (Article 326)
  • First-past-the-post system
  • Delimitation based on population (last freeze till 2026)

Presiding Officers

PostDetails
SpeakerElected by members of Lok Sabha, maintains order & discipline

Pro-Tem SpeakerTemporary, appointed to conduct first session post-election

Important Powers of Lok Sabha

  • Money Bills: Can only be introduced in Lok Sabha (Article 110)
  • No-Confidence Motion: Passed only in Lok Sabha
  • Budget Presentation: Done exclusively in Lok Sabha
  • Controls the Executive: Through Questions, Debates, Motions

Sessions of Lok Sabha

  • Budget Session (Feb–May)
  • Monsoon Session (July–Sep)
  • Winter Session (Nov–Dec)
  • Called by President under Article 85

Special Terms

  • Zero Hour: Informal, unlisted questions after Question Hour
  • Question Hour: First hour of every sitting for asking government accountability
  • Calling Attention Motion: To draw attention to urgent public matters

Important Articles Related to Lok Sabha

  • Article 81: Composition of Lok Sabha
  • Article 83: Duration of Houses
  • Article 93: Speaker and Deputy Speaker
  • Article 110: Money Bill

Did You Know?

  • First Lok Sabha election held in 1952
  • Speaker can disqualify members under Anti-Defection Law (10th Schedule)
  • 2 Anglo-Indian seats were removed by 104th Amendment Act, 2019
