President vs Governor

“While the President is the head of the entire Union, the Governor acts as the President’s shadow in the states.” — Bibliotech
Feature President of India Governor of a State
Article Article 52 to 62 Article 153 to 162
Appointed By Elected by Electoral College Appointed by the President
Term 5 years 5 years (but holds office at President’s pleasure)
Qualification Citizen of India, 35+ years, eligible for LS Citizen of India, 35+ years, eligible for LS
Removal Impeachment by Parliament Can be removed by President anytime
Oath Administered By Chief Justice of India Chief Justice of High Court
Executive Powers Appoints PM, Ministers, Governors, Judges etc. Appoints CM, State Ministers, State officials
Legislative Powers Summons & dissolves Parliament, gives assent Summons & dissolves State Assembly, assent to Bills
Judicial Powers Pardoning powers under Article 72 Pardoning powers under Article 161 (only for state laws)
Emergency Powers Can proclaim National, State & Financial Emergency No such power
Position De jure Head of the Union De jure Head of the State
Discretionary Powers Minimal (used only in constitutional crisis) More frequent use (e.g., when no party gets majority)
