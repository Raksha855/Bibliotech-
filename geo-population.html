Population & Migration

"Population is the engine of the economy; migration is its fuel." — Bibliotech

What is Population?

Population refers to the total number of people inhabiting a country, region, or planet. In geography, it involves the study of its size, growth rate, distribution, and its impacts on resources and the environment.

Population Density

Population density is the measure of the number of people living per unit of area, typically per square kilometer or square mile.

  • Formula: Population Density = Total Population / Area
  • High Density Areas: Example – Ganga-Yamuna plains
  • Low Density Areas: Example – Rajasthan, deserts

Factors Affecting Population Distribution

  • Physical Factors: Climate, soil, water bodies
  • Economic Factors: Availability of employment, industries
  • Political Factors: Government policies, migration laws
  • Social Factors: Education, healthcare, living standards

Population Growth & Demographic Transition

  • Growth Rate: Difference between birth rate and death rate
  • Stages of Demographic Transition:
    • Stage 1: High birth & death rates (Pre-industrial society)
    • Stage 2: High birth rate, falling death rate (Developing countries)
    • Stage 3: Low birth & death rates (Developed countries)

Migration

Migration refers to the movement of people from one location to another, either temporarily or permanently. It is primarily driven by social, economic, political, and environmental factors.

Types of Migration

  • Internal Migration: Movement within a country (e.g., rural to urban migration)
  • International Migration: Movement across countries (e.g., labor migration, refugee movement)

Push & Pull Factors

  • Push Factors: Poverty, unemployment, environmental hazards
  • Pull Factors: Job opportunities, better living conditions, education

Migration Trends in India

  • Rural-to-Urban Migration: Movement from villages to cities for better economic opportunities
  • Interstate Migration: Movement between states for work, education
  • International Migration: Indian diaspora in Middle East, USA, Canada

Urbanization

Urbanization refers to the increasing population in urban areas, resulting in the expansion of cities and towns.

  • Causes of Urbanization: Industrialization, better living conditions, social facilities
  • Consequences of Urbanization: Overpopulation, pollution, strain on resources
