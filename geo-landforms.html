Landforms & Geomorphology

"Landforms are the Earth’s memory — carved by time, shaped by force." — Bibliotech

What is Geomorphology?

Geomorphology is the scientific study of landforms and the processes that shape them. It includes both endogenic (internal) and exogenic (external) forces that mold the Earth's surface.

Classification of Landforms

  • Primary: Continental & oceanic features (crustal movement)
  • Secondary: Mountains, plateaus, plains (from tectonic forces)
  • Tertiary: Features formed by erosion and deposition

Agents of Landform Formation

  • Endogenic: Tectonic uplift, volcanism, earthquakes
  • Exogenic: River, wind, glacier, sea waves, weathering

Landforms by Running Water (Fluvial)

Process Landforms
Erosion V-shaped valley, gorges, canyons, waterfalls
Deposition Alluvial fans, floodplains, levees, deltas, meanders

Landforms by Glaciers (Glacial)

  • Erosional: Cirque, Arete, Horn, U-shaped valley
  • Depositional: Moraines, Drumlin, Eskers

Landforms by Wind (Aeolian)

  • Erosional: Yardangs, Deflation hollows, Mushroom rocks
  • Depositional: Dunes, Loess

Landforms by Sea Waves (Marine)

  • Erosional: Sea cliffs, arches, stacks
  • Depositional: Beaches, spits, bars, lagoons
