The Mughal Empire (1526–1857)

"The grandeur of the Mughals lay not just in power, but in the art of administration." — Bibliotech

Founding of the Empire

  • Founded by **Babur** after defeating **Ibrahim Lodi** in the **First Battle of Panipat (1526)**.
  • Babur was a descendant of Timur (father’s side) and Genghis Khan (mother’s side).

Important Battles

  • First Battle of Panipat (1526): Babur vs. Ibrahim Lodi
  • Battle of Khanwa (1527): Babur vs. Rana Sanga
  • Battle of Ghaghra (1529): Babur vs. Afghan Chiefs
  • Second Battle of Panipat (1556): Akbar vs. Hemu (Regained Delhi)

Administrative System

  • Mansabdari System: Rank-based military-cum-bureaucratic system started by Akbar.
  • Zabt System: Land revenue assessment based on crop productivity.
  • Centralized Governance: Emperor was the supreme authority; supported by Diwan, Mir Bakshi, Sadr, etc.

Art & Architecture

  • Fusion of Persian, Indian and Central Asian styles.
  • Famous monuments: Humayun’s Tomb, Red Fort, Jama Masjid, Taj Mahal.
  • Miniature paintings flourished under Jahangir.

Religion & Society

  • Akbar's **Sulh-i-kul** (peace with all) policy encouraged religious tolerance.
  • Din-i-Ilahi — spiritual initiative by Akbar (limited followers).
  • Aurangzeb reimposed jizya and banned music/dance in court.

Decline of Mughal Empire

  • After Aurangzeb’s death, weak successors and internal rebellions (Jats, Sikhs, Marathas).
  • Invasions by **Nadir Shah (1739)** and **Ahmad Shah Abdali** weakened empire.
  • British expansion post-1757 further reduced Mughal authority.
  • Bahadur Shah Zafar, last emperor, exiled after Revolt of 1857.

UPSC Relevance

  • Prelims: Battles, monuments, Mansabdari, Din-i-Ilahi, Baburnama
  • Mains GS1: Mughal administration, religion & art, causes of decline
  • Essay: “The Mughal legacy in modern Indian governance”
