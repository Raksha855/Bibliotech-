National Income Accounting

"National Income tells us not just how much we earn as a country, but how wisely we grow together." — Bibliotech

What is National Income?

National Income is the total monetary value of all final goods and services produced by the residents of a country in a financial year. It reflects the economic health of a nation.

Key Concepts

  • GDP (Gross Domestic Product): Total value of goods/services produced within the geographical boundary of a country.
  • GNP (Gross National Product): GDP + Income earned from abroad by residents - income earned by foreigners within the country.
  • NNP (Net National Product): GNP – Depreciation
  • National Income (at factor cost): NNP at factor cost (includes income earned by factors of production).
  • Personal Income: Total income actually received by individuals.
  • Disposable Income: Income remaining after direct taxes are deducted.

Three Methods of Measuring National Income

Method Description Formula
Production Method Sum of value added by all producing units Gross Value Added = Output – Intermediate Consumption
Income Method Sum of incomes earned by factors of production NI = Rent + Wages + Interest + Profits + Mixed Income
Expenditure Method Sum of all expenditures made in an economy NI = C + I + G + (X - M)

Important Terms

  • Depreciation: Reduction in the value of capital goods over time due to wear and tear.
  • Net Factor Income from Abroad (NFIA): Income earned from abroad minus income paid to foreigners.
  • Subsidies: Government support to reduce the cost of production.
  • Indirect Taxes: Taxes like GST, Excise Duty etc.

UPSC Relevance

  • Conceptual clarity is tested in both Prelims and Mains (GS Paper 3)
  • Used in understanding economic surveys, budgets & current affairs
  • Frequently appears in statements in objective questions (e.g., GDP vs GNP)
