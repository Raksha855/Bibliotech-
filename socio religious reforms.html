Economic Impact of British Rule in India

“They came as traders, they ruled as governors, and left a legacy of economic transformation.” — Bibliotech

1. Drain of Wealth

  • Concept: Transfer of Indian resources to Britain without adequate return.
  • Mechanisms: Home Charges, military expenditure, pensions, interest on debt.
  • Result: Annual drain estimated at 5–6% of GDP by Dadabhai Naoroji.

2. Deindustrialization

  • Decline of Textile Industry: Cheap machine-made British textiles flooded Indian markets.
  • Collapse of Artisan Classes: Weavers, metalworkers, craftsmen lost patronage & markets.
  • Shift to Raw Material Export: India became supplier of cotton, jute, opium.

3. Land Revenue Systems

SystemRegionFeatures & Impact
Permanent Settlement (1793) Bengal, Bihar, Odisha Fixed revenue to Zamindars; peasant exploitation; zamindari absenteeism
Ryotwari System Madras, Bombay Presidencies Assessment directly on peasants; high rates; frequent defaults & indebtedness
Mahalwari System Punjab, NW Provinces Village/estate‐level settlements; collective liability; uneven implementation

4. Famine & Agrarian Distress

  • Policies: Cash crops encouraged; neglect of foodgrains cultivation.
  • Famines: Major famines in 1770, 1860s, 1876–78, 1899–1900, 1943.
  • Relief Measures: Ad hoc & inadequate; laissez-faire ideology worsened mortality.

5. Infrastructure & Railways

  • Railway network laid primarily to serve British economic interests & troop movement.
  • Investment by British capital; heavy loans servicing burden on Indian revenue.
  • Limited industrial backward linkages; railways did not spur Indian industrialization significantly.

6. Commercialization & Urbanization

  • Growth of port cities: Bombay, Calcutta, Madras as export hubs.
  • Emergence of new merchant classes: Marwaris, Chettiars, Parsis.
  • Gradual urban growth around railheads and administrative centers.

7. Economic Legacies

  • Land tenure patterns & rural indebtedness persisting post-Independence.
  • Railway infrastructure foundational but oriented to colonial needs.
  • Commercial agriculture established cash-crop dominance.
  • Regional economic disparities deepened between industrializing West & agrarian East.

UPSC Relevance

  • Prelims: Systems (Permanent Settlement, Ryotwari), famines, Drain theory.
  • Mains GS1/GS3: Impact of colonial economic policies, agrarian distress, infrastructure.
  • Essay: “The economics of Empire: Assessing British rule in India.”
