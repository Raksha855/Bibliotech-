Introduction to Economics

“Economics is the art of making the most out of life with limited resources.” — Bibliotech

What is Economics?

Economics is the social science that studies how individuals, governments, firms, and nations make choices on allocating limited resources to satisfy their unlimited wants.

Key Definitions

  • Adam Smith (1776): "Economics is the science of wealth."
  • Alfred Marshall: "Economics is the study of mankind in the ordinary business of life."
  • Paul Samuelson: "Economics is the study of how people and society choose to use scarce resources."

Branches of Economics

Branch Focus
Microeconomics Study of individual units like consumers and firms
Macroeconomics Study of economy-wide phenomena like GDP, inflation
Development Economics Focus on improving living standards in underdeveloped economies
Public Economics Study of government revenue and expenditure policies

Central Problem of Economics

Every economy faces three fundamental questions:

  • What to produce?
  • How to produce?
  • For whom to produce?

Why Economics is Important for UPSC?

  • Helps understand policy decisions like Budget, GST, Monetary Policy
  • Supports answers in GS Paper 3 and Essay
  • Essential for understanding Current Affairs
