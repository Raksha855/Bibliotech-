Mauryan Empire

"From the sword of Chandragupta to the dhamma of Ashoka — India witnessed her first great empire." — Bibliotech

Introduction

The Mauryan Empire (322–185 BCE) was the first large-scale empire in Indian history. Founded by Chandragupta Maurya with the guidance of Chanakya (Kautilya), the empire reached its zenith under Ashoka the Great.

Chandragupta Maurya (322–298 BCE)

  • Overthrew Nanda dynasty with help of Chanakya
  • Defeated Seleucus I (Greek) — signed treaty, received territory + Megasthenes as ambassador
  • Capital: Pataliputra
  • Retired to Karnataka (Jain monk at Sravanabelagola)

Megasthenes' Account (Indica)

  • Describes Mauryan administration, army, society
  • Mentions seven classes: philosophers, farmers, soldiers, herdsmen, artisans, magistrates, councillors

Bindusara (298–273 BCE)

  • Son of Chandragupta, known as "Amitraghata" (slayer of enemies)
  • Extended empire to Deccan (except Kalinga)
  • Maintained diplomatic relations with Syria, Egypt, Greece

Ashoka the Great (273–232 BCE)

  • Fought the Kalinga War (261 BCE) — ~1,00,000 deaths
  • Moved by suffering, embraced Buddhism
  • Promoted Dhamma (ethical life) instead of conquest

Features of Ashoka's Rule

  • Appointed Dhamma Mahamattas to spread moral teachings
  • Banned animal sacrifices, promoted religious tolerance
  • Built roads, rest houses, planted trees
  • Promoted Buddhism: sent missions to Sri Lanka (his son Mahendra & daughter Sanghamitra)

Ashokan Edicts

  • 14 Major Rock Edicts, 7 Pillar Edicts, Minor Edicts
  • Languages: Prakrit, Greek, Aramaic
  • Scripts: Brahmi (India), Kharosthi (NW), Greek, Aramaic
  • Locations: Girnar, Kalsi, Dhauli, Sarnath, Sanchi, etc.

Administration under Mauryas

  • Highly centralized bureaucratic state
  • Arthashastra (by Chanakya) — detailed manual on governance, spies, economy, war
  • Provinces ruled by royal princes (Kumara)
  • Efficient taxation, trade, irrigation system

Military Strength

  • Large standing army (600,000 infantry, 9,000 elephants, 30,000 cavalry — per Megasthenes)
  • Spies & informers — secret service mentioned in Arthashastra

Decline of Mauryan Empire

  • Weak successors after Ashoka
  • Over-centralization & huge military expense
  • Invasion by Bactrian Greeks (Indo-Greeks)
  • Last ruler: Brihadratha killed by his general Pushyamitra Shunga (185 BCE)
