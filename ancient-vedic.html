Vedic Age

"The Vedas were not just scriptures — they were the soul of a civilization." — Bibliotech

Timeline & Division

  • Early Vedic Age: 1500–1000 BCE (Rigvedic Period)
  • Later Vedic Age: 1000–600 BCE (Samaveda, Yajurveda, Atharvaveda period)

1. Sources of Vedic Age

  • Rigveda – earliest literary source (1028 hymns)
  • Other Vedas – Samaveda, Yajurveda, Atharvaveda
  • Brahmanas, Aranyakas, Upanishads
  • Epics: Ramayana & Mahabharata (composed later)

2. Early Vedic Age (Rigvedic Period)

Political Structure

  • Tribal polity — no large kingdoms
  • Key tribes: Bharatas, Purus, Yadus, Druhyus
  • Institutions: Sabha, Samiti, Gana, Vidatha
  • Rajan (king), Purohit (priest), Senani (army chief)

Society

  • Patriarchal and tribal
  • Women had respectable position — could attend Sabha, compose hymns
  • No rigid varna system

Economy

  • Pastoral economy — cattle main wealth (word "Gavya")
  • Barter system — cow as unit of exchange
  • No coins yet

Religion

  • Nature worship: Indra (war), Agni (fire), Varuna (cosmic order)
  • No temples or idol worship
  • Yajnas (sacrifices) were common

Literature

  • Rigveda: Collection of hymns in praise of deities
  • Language: Vedic Sanskrit
  • Oral tradition through Shruti (heard) and Smriti (remembered)

3. Later Vedic Age (1000–600 BCE)

Political Evolution

  • Emergence of **mahajanapadas** and kingdoms (e.g., Kuru, Panchala)
  • Rajan became more powerful — hereditary monarchy
  • Offices became institutionalized: Rajan, Senani, Purohit

Social Changes

  • Varna system became rigid: Brahmin, Kshatriya, Vaishya, Shudra
  • Women's status declined — barred from education and rituals
  • Gotra and ashrama systems evolved

Economic Developments

  • Agriculture became mainstay
  • Use of iron tools (krishna ayas)
  • Settled village life emerged
  • Trade began — Nishka and Satamana used as currency (not coins)

Religion & Philosophy

  • Complex rituals and sacrifices
  • Brahmanas and Upanishads composed
  • Rise of **spiritual philosophy** — karma, rebirth, moksha

Literary Developments

  • Samaveda — musical chants
  • Yajurveda — sacrificial formulae
  • Atharvaveda — daily rituals and spells
  • Later: Aranyakas and Upanishads (philosophical texts)

Comparison Table: Early vs Later Vedic

Aspect Early Vedic Later Vedic
Polity Tribal assemblies Hereditary monarchy
Economy Pastoral, cattle rearing Agriculture dominant
Society Less varna rigidity Rigid caste system
Women Respected, educated Decline in status
Religion Nature worship Ritualism + philosophy
