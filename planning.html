Planning in India

"A plan without action is a daydream; action without a plan is a nightmare." — Bibliotech

What is Economic Planning?

Economic planning is the process through which a country defines its economic goals and strategically allocates its resources to achieve them. In India, planning was implemented through Five-Year Plans (1951–2017) and is now carried out by NITI Aayog.

Types of Planning

  • Indicative Planning: Non-compulsory, sets general economic goals (used now)
  • Directive Planning: Strict state control, targets and allocation (used earlier)

Planning Commission (1950–2014)

  • Created by executive resolution (not constitutional)
  • Prepared Five-Year Plans
  • First Chairman: Jawaharlal Nehru

Important Five-Year Plans Summary

Plan Years Focus
1st Plan 1951–56 Agriculture, irrigation
2nd Plan 1956–61 Heavy industries (Nehru-Mahalanobis model)
5th Plan 1974–79 Garibi Hatao & self-reliance
8th Plan 1992–97 Liberalization & growth
11th Plan 2007–12 Inclusive growth
12th Plan 2012–17 Faster, sustainable, inclusive growth

NITI Aayog (Since 2015)

  • Full Form: National Institution for Transforming India
  • Think tank and policy guide for government
  • Promotes cooperative federalism
  • Prepares vision documents (e.g., Strategy @75)
  • CEO + Vice-Chairperson + Governing Council

Achievements of Planning

  • Green Revolution
  • Industrial base development
  • Poverty alleviation programs
  • Infrastructure expansion

Limitations

  • Lack of implementation efficiency
  • Unequal regional development
  • Too much centralization
  • Poor private sector participation

UPSC Relevance
