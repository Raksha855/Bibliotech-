Meaning of Terms in the Preamble

1. Sovereign

India is internally and externally sovereign – externally free from foreign control and internally has a government elected by the people.

2. Socialist

Emphasizes social and economic equality. Not in Marxist sense, but a democratic socialism that seeks to end poverty and reduce income inequality.

3. Secular

The state has no official religion and treats all religions equally. Ensures freedom of religion for all individuals.

4. Democratic

Power rests with the people who elect their representatives through free and fair elections. Includes political, social, and economic democracy.

5. Republic

India has an elected head of state – the President. This signifies an end to hereditary monarchy and promotes equality.

6. Justice

Includes Social, Economic, and Political justice. It ensures fair treatment without discrimination of caste, creed, or gender.

7. Liberty

Guarantees liberty of thought, expression, belief, faith and worship to all citizens, under Article 19 to 22 of the Constitution.

8. Equality

All citizens are equal before law (Article 14) and have equal protection of laws, opportunities and status.

9. Fraternity

Promotes a sense of brotherhood among all Indians. It assures the dignity of the individual and unity & integrity of the nation.
