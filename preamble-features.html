Preamble to the Constitution of India

Introduction

The Preamble is the introductory statement to the Constitution of India. It sets out the guiding principles and ideals that the Constitution seeks to achieve. It declares India as a Sovereign, Socialist, Secular, Democratic Republic, ensuring Justice, Liberty, Equality, and Fraternity to all its citizens.

Features of the Preamble

  • Sovereign – India is a sovereign country, free to make its own decisions without interference from outside.
  • Socialist – The state aims for economic equality and seeks to remove the rich-poor divide.
  • Secular – The state does not favor any religion over others and ensures equal treatment for all religions.
  • Democratic – The government is formed by elected representatives of the people, and the power lies with the people.
  • Republic – The head of state (President) is elected, not a hereditary monarch.
