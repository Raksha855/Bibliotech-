Parliament of India – A Complete UPSC Overview

“Parliament is not merely a law-making body; it is the living soul of a representative democracy.” — Bibliotech

What is Parliament?

Established under Article 79, Parliament is the supreme law-making institution in India. It includes:

  • President of India
  • Rajya Sabha (Upper House)
  • Lok Sabha (Lower House)

Composition of Parliament

ComponentDetails
PresidentPart of Parliament, summons sessions
Rajya Sabha245 members (Upper House)
Lok Sabha545 members (Lower House)

Lok Sabha – House of the People

  • Maximum strength: 552 (now 545)
  • Tenure: 5 years
  • Presiding Officer: Speaker (Article 93)

Rajya Sabha – Council of States

  • Permanent body (not dissolved)
  • 1/3rd retires every 2 years
  • Chairperson: Vice President
  • Can pass resolutions on State List (Article 249)

Functions of Parliament

  • Legislative: Laws on Union & Concurrent List
  • Executive Control: Questions, Motions, No-confidence
  • Financial: Budget, Appropriation Bills
  • Judicial: Impeachment, inquiries

Anti-Defection Law

Part of 10th Schedule (1985) to prevent political defections. Disqualification handled by Speaker/Chairperson.

Privileges (Article 105)

  • Freedom of speech in Parliament
  • No legal liability for anything said during session
  • Limited protection from arrest
