External Sector & Balance of Payments (BoP)

"A nation’s external sector is the window to its global economic strength." — Bibliotech

What is the External Sector?

The external sector deals with a country’s economic transactions with the rest of the world, including trade (exports/imports), foreign investment, remittances, and exchange rate policy.

Key Components of External Sector

  • Foreign Trade: Exports & Imports of goods/services
  • Foreign Investment: FDI & FPI
  • Foreign Exchange Reserves: Held by RBI
  • Exchange Rate Management: Managed float system in India

What is Balance of Payments (BoP)?

BoP is a statement that summarizes economic transactions between residents of a country and the rest of the world during a given period.

BoP Accounts

Account Includes Balance Meaning
Current Account Trade in goods/services, remittances, income Surplus/deficit indicates export-import balance
Capital Account FDI, FPI, ECBs, NRI deposits Reflects capital inflow/outflow
Errors & Omissions Adjusts statistical mismatches Ensures total BoP = 0
Foreign Exchange Reserves Maintained by RBI to handle crisis & stability Change = BoP deficit/surplus

Terms You Must Know

  • BoT (Balance of Trade): Exports – Imports of goods only
  • Current Account Deficit (CAD): Negative current account balance
  • FDI vs FPI: Direct = long-term control, Portfolio = short-term investment
  • Forex Reserves: Gold, foreign currency assets, SDRs, reserve position in IMF

India’s Forex Trends (Recent UPSC Angle)

  • India’s forex reserves crossed $600 billion in 2023
  • Current Account Deficit fluctuated due to crude oil imports
  • FDI stable, FPI volatile due to global factors
