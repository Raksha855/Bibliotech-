Right to Equality (Articles 14–18)

The Right to Equality is the foundation of a democratic state. It ensures equal treatment of all individuals before the law and prohibits discrimination.

Article 14 – Equality before Law and Equal Protection of Laws

  • “The State shall not deny to any person equality before the law or the equal protection of the laws within the territory of India.”
  • Equality before law – British origin – absence of any special privilege.
  • Equal protection of laws – American origin – equal treatment of equals.
  • Case: E.P. Royappa vs State of Tamil Nadu – Equality is a dynamic concept.

Article 15 – Prohibition of Discrimination

  • State shall not discriminate against any citizen on grounds of religion, race, caste, sex, or place of birth.
  • Permits special provisions for women, children, socially and educationally backward classes (SEBCs), SCs, STs.
  • Case: Indra Sawhney vs Union of India – Reservation is not violation of equality.

Article 16 – Equality of Opportunity in Public Employment

  • Guarantees equality of opportunity in matters of public employment or appointment to any office under the State.
  • Allows reservation for backward classes not adequately represented.

Article 17 – Abolition of Untouchability

  • Untouchability is abolished and its practice in any form is forbidden.
  • It is punishable under the Protection of Civil Rights Act, 1955.

Article 18 – Abolition of Titles

  • State shall not confer any titles except military or academic distinctions.
  • Prohibits Indian citizens from accepting titles from foreign states.
  • Case: Balaji Raghavan vs Union of India – Civilian awards like Bharat Ratna are not titles.
