Union Budget & Fiscal Policy

"A Budget is not just numbers, it's a reflection of national priorities." — Bibliotech

What is Union Budget?

The Union Budget is the annual financial statement of the Government of India, presented under Article 112 of the Constitution. It estimates government’s receipts and expenditures for the financial year (April 1 – March 31).

Structure of the Budget

  • Revenue Budget: Includes revenue receipts and revenue expenditure.
  • Capital Budget: Includes capital receipts and capital expenditure.

Components of Government Receipts

Type Includes
Revenue Receipts Tax Revenue + Non-Tax Revenue (Interest, Dividends, etc.)
Capital Receipts Borrowings, Disinvestment, Recovery of Loans

Components of Government Expenditure

Type Includes
Revenue Expenditure Salaries, Subsidies, Pensions, Interest Payments
Capital Expenditure Infrastructure, Asset Creation, Loan Disbursal

Types of Budgets

  • Balanced Budget: Receipts = Expenditure
  • Surplus Budget: Receipts > Expenditure
  • Deficit Budget: Expenditure > Receipts (Common in India)

What is Fiscal Policy?

Fiscal Policy refers to the use of government spending and tax policies to influence the economy. It can be expansionary or contractionary depending on economic conditions.

FRBM Act, 2003

  • FRBM: Fiscal Responsibility and Budget Management Act
  • Aims to maintain fiscal discipline and reduce fiscal deficit
  • Set targets: 3% Fiscal Deficit, 0% Revenue Deficit (originally)
