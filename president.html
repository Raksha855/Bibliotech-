President of India

“The President is the ceremonial head of state and the constitutional guardian of Indian democracy.” — Bibliotech

What is the President?

The President of India is the head of the Indian State and the highest constitutional authority under Article 52. The President acts as the nominal executive, with real powers vested in the Council of Ministers headed by the Prime Minister.

Key Articles

  • Article 52: There shall be a President of India
  • Article 53: Executive power of the Union
  • Article 54–62: Election, qualifications, term, and impeachment
  • Article 72: Pardoning power

Election of the President

  • Elected by an electoral college: MPs (both Houses) + MLAs (of states + UTs with legislature)
  • Voting method: Proportional Representation with Single Transferable Vote (STV)
  • Qualification: Indian citizen, 35+ years, eligible for LS
  • Term: 5 years (can resign or be impeached)

Oath

Administered by the Chief Justice of India under Article 60.

Impeachment (Article 61)

  • Violation of the Constitution
  • Impeachment resolution passed by 2/3rd majority of both Houses
  • Detailed quasi-judicial process

Powers of the President

Executive Powers

  • Appoints PM, Ministers, Governors, Judges of SC/HC, etc.
  • Supreme Commander of the Armed Forces

Legislative Powers

  • Summons, prorogues & dissolves Parliament
  • Nominates 12 RS members & 2 Anglo-Indians (now repealed)
  • Assents to Bills, can return (except Money Bill)
  • Can promulgate Ordinances (Article 123)

Judicial Powers

  • Pardoning powers under Article 72:
    • Pardon
    • Reprieve
    • Remit
    • Commute
    • Respite

Emergency Powers

  • Article 352: National Emergency
  • Article 356: President’s Rule
  • Article 360: Financial Emergency

Important Notes

  • Acts on advice of Council of Ministers (Article 74)
  • Bound to follow Cabinet advice after 44th Amendment
  • Can use discretion only in few cases (e.g., hung parliament)
