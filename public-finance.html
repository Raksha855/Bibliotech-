Public Finance

"Public finance is the mirror of a nation’s economic intentions and responsibilities." — Bibliotech

What is Public Finance?

Public finance deals with the income and expenditure of government and how it affects the economy. It plays a crucial role in resource allocation, income redistribution, and economic stability.

Main Components of Public Finance

  • Public Revenue: Income from taxes, fees, fines, dividends, etc.
  • Public Expenditure: Spending by government on welfare, defense, infrastructure, subsidies, etc.
  • Public Debt: Borrowing by government from internal and external sources.
  • Fiscal Policy: Government's use of revenue and expenditure to influence the economy.

Types of Taxes

Type Description Examples
Direct Tax Paid directly by individuals/institutions to govt. Income Tax, Corporate Tax
Indirect Tax Collected by intermediaries on behalf of govt. GST, Customs Duty

Types of Government Expenditure

  • Capital Expenditure: Spent on creating assets (e.g. infrastructure)
  • Revenue Expenditure: Recurring expenses like salaries, subsidies
  • Planned & Non-Planned: (Now merged in budget classification)

Types of Deficits

Deficit Formula Meaning
Fiscal Deficit Total Expenditure – Total Receipts (Excl. borrowings) Shows overall borrowing requirement of govt.
Revenue Deficit Revenue Expenditure – Revenue Receipts Indicates dissaving of govt.
Primary Deficit Fiscal Deficit – Interest Payments Reflects fiscal deficit excluding past interest dues
