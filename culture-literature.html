Culture & Literature in Medieval India

"Where the pen met the temple bells and the sword bowed to art, India's medieval age danced in poetry and prayer." — Bibliotech

Introduction

The culture of medieval India was a beautiful tapestry woven with threads of religion, philosophy, literature, music, art, and architecture. It reflected a unique **Indo-Islamic synthesis**, shaped by **Sultanate rulers**, **Mughals**, **Bhakti & Sufi saints**, and **regional dynasties**.

Language & Literature

Persian Literature

  • Official language of the Delhi Sultanate & Mughal courts.
  • Amir Khusrau: Father of Qawwali; wrote in Persian & Hindavi; coined terms like “Hindustani.”
  • Famous works: *Tughlaq Nama*, *Khazain-ul-Futuh*, *Nuh Sipihr*.
  • Abul Fazl’s Akbarnama & Ain-i-Akbari — detailed Mughal administration & culture.

Regional Literature

  • Hindi: Tulsidas (Ramcharitmanas), Surdas (Krishna Bhakti), Kabir (Dohe).
  • Marathi: Dnyaneshwar, Namdev — Bhakti poetry.
  • Bengali: Chaitanya Mahaprabhu’s Vaishnav compositions.
  • Kannada & Telugu: Vijayanagara court poetry; Allasani Peddana was court poet of Krishna Deva Raya.
  • Punjabi: Guru Nanak’s Bani — Adi Granth compilation.

Urdu Emergence

  • Fusion of Persian + Turkish + Hindi + Arabic.
  • Popular in North India, especially post-Delhi Sultanate era.
  • Urdu poetry, ghazals, and storytelling (Dastan tradition) grew under Nawabs.

Religious Literature

  • Bhakti Movement: Spiritual poems in regional languages — accessible to masses.
  • Sufi Saints: Qawwalis, couplets (shayari), mystical Persian literature.
  • Vaishnava Literature: Devotional works dedicated to Vishnu/Krishna.

Music & Performing Arts

Classical Music

  • Hindustani music evolved: Synthesized Vedic, folk, and Persian music.
  • Instruments: Sitar, Tabla, Sarod, Santoor, Pakhawaj, Veena.
  • Amir Khusrau: Credited with Sitar and Qawwali development.

Dance Forms

  • Temples continued to support Bharatanatyam, Kathakali, and Odissi.
  • Kathak: Transformed from temple storytelling to court dance during Mughal period.

Drama & Folk Culture

  • Yakshagana (Karnataka), Jatra (Bengal), Nautanki (UP), Tamasha (Maharashtra): Popular folk theatres.
  • Stories from Ramayana, Mahabharata, and local legends.

Painting & Decorative Arts

  • Mughal Miniature Paintings: Portraits, court scenes, flora-fauna, battles.
  • Rajput Paintings: Bold colors, religious themes, romanticism (Rajasthani & Pahari styles).
  • Persian Influence: Realism, garden scenes, and attention to detail.

Patronage of Culture

  • Delhi Sultanate: Promoted Persian literature, mosque architecture, Sufi culture.
  • Mughal Empire: Art, architecture, poetry, music — peaked under Akbar, Jahangir, Shah Jahan.
  • Vijayanagara: Promoted temple arts, dance, Sanskrit & regional literature.
  • Bhakti & Sufi Saints: Carried art to the masses through oral tradition.

Key Cultural Centers

Region Contribution
Delhi & Agra Persian literature, music, painting, Mughal court culture
Varanasi Bhakti poetry, classical music, temple art
Gujarat Textile, architecture, Jain manuscripts
Kolkata, Bengal Vaishnav poetry, folk art, trade influence
Vijayanagara South Indian dance, sculpture, Sanskrit poetry
