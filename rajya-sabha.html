Rajya Sabha – The Council of States

“Rajya Sabha ensures that Indian federalism flows from the wisdom of the States.” — Bibliotech

Introduction

Rajya Sabha is the **Upper House of the Indian Parliament**, representing the States of India at the national level. It ensures federal character and acts as a revising chamber for laws.

Composition

  • Total Strength: 250 (as per Constitution)
  • Current Strength: 245 members (233 elected + 12 nominated)
  • 12 Nominated Members: By President – experts from art, literature, science, and social service

Election Process

  • 233 members elected by elected members of State Legislative Assemblies
  • Method: **Proportional Representation by Single Transferable Vote**
  • 12 members nominated by President

Tenure

  • Rajya Sabha is a **permanent body**; cannot be dissolved
  • 1/3rd members retire every 2 years
  • Each member serves a **6-year term**

Presiding Officers

Special Powers of Rajya Sabha

  • Article 249: Can allow Parliament to make laws on State List (2/3rd majority required)
  • Article 312: Can authorize creation of All-India Services
  • Acts as a stabilizing force when Lok Sabha is dissolved

Functions of Rajya Sabha

  • Legislative function (except Money Bills)
  • Deliberative chamber – debates & discussions
  • Approves Constitutional Amendments
  • Participates in election of President & Vice President

Limitations

  • No control over Council of Ministers
  • Cannot initiate or amend Money Bills
  • Speaker of Lok Sabha decides whether a bill is Money Bill

Important Articles

  • Article 80: Composition of Rajya Sabha
  • Article 84: Qualifications for membership
  • Article 249: Power to legislate on State List
  • Article 312: Creation of All India Services

Trivia

  • First sitting: 13 May 1952
  • Called the “Upper House” but does not imply superiority
  • Acts as a revising body to check rushed legislations
