Art, Literature & Science in Ancient India

"Civilizations are remembered by their art, preserved by their scripts, and immortalized by their discoveries." — Bibliotech

PART 1: Art & Architecture

1. Prehistoric Art

  • Bhimbetka Cave Paintings (Madhya Pradesh) — over 500 caves with Mesolithic paintings
  • Depicts hunting, dancing, animals — painted with red ochre and white chalk
  • Shows early man’s aesthetic sensibility and life activities

2. Indus Valley Architecture

  • Urban Planning: Grid layout, drainage system, brick houses
  • Important Sites: Harappa, Mohenjo-Daro, Dholavira, Kalibangan
  • Great Bath (Mohenjo-Daro): Early example of ritual architecture
  • Use of burnt bricks, granaries, citadels, and public platforms

3. Mauryan Art

  • Ashokan Pillars: Monolithic polished stone pillars with animal capitals
  • Lion Capital of Sarnath: National Emblem of India
  • Use of Mauryan polish, influence of Persian Achaemenid art
  • Barabar Caves: Rock-cut caves for Ajivika sect (earliest example of rock-cut architecture)

4. Buddhist Art

  • Stupas: Dome-shaped structures for Buddhist relics — Sanchi, Amaravati, Bharhut
  • Chaityas & Viharas: Prayer halls and monasteries — Karle, Ajanta
  • Rock-cut Architecture: Ellora, Ajanta, Nagarjunakonda

5. Gupta Art (Classical Age)

  • Harmony, proportion, idealized human form
  • Ajanta Caves: Buddhist frescoes with graceful lines & naturalism
  • Dashavatara Temple, Deogarh: Early structural temple (Vaishnavite)
  • Use of Shikhara, Garbhagriha, Mandapa in temples

6. Post-Gupta Temple Styles

  • Nagara Style: North Indian, curved Shikhara — Khajuraho temples
  • Dravidian Style: South Indian, pyramid shape — Brihadeshwara temple
  • Vesara Style: Deccan mix of both — Pattadakal temples

PART 2: Literature in Ancient India

"When stones were silent, words sang — India’s soul echoed through its scriptures." — Bibliotech

1. Vedic Literature

  • Vedas: Oldest scriptures of mankind (1500–500 BCE)
  • Four Vedas: Rigveda, Samaveda, Yajurveda, Atharvaveda
  • Rigveda: Oldest, hymns dedicated to nature gods
  • Brahmanas – Rituals | Aranyakas – Forest texts | Upanishads – Philosophical thoughts

2. Epics

  • Mahabharata: Longest epic in the world — 1 lakh shlokas, by Ved Vyasa
  • Ramayana: Story of Rama, ideal rule and dharma — by Valmiki
  • Ethical, moral, political guidance — also used in Bhakti literature

3. Buddhist & Jain Literature

  • Tripitakas: Vinaya, Sutta, Abhidhamma — in Pali
  • Jatakas: Stories of previous births of Buddha
  • Jain Agamas: Teachings of Mahavira — written in Prakrit
  • Bhadrabahu: Early Jain monk; Hemachandra later codified Jain texts

4. Sanskrit Literature (Classical Age)

  • Kalidasa: India’s greatest poet-dramatist
  • Plays: Abhijnanasakuntalam, Malavikagnimitram
  • Poems: Meghadutam, Raghuvamsa, Kumarasambhava

5. Grammar & Linguistics

  • Panini: Author of Ashtadhyayi — most scientific grammar of Sanskrit (c. 4th century BCE)
  • Patanjali: Wrote Mahabhashya on Panini's grammar
  • Katyayana: Commentator on grammar and linguistics

6. Arthashastra

  • Written by Kautilya (Chanakya) — prime minister of Chandragupta Maurya
  • Manual on statecraft, economics, war strategy, and administration

7. Literature in Regional Languages (Early)

  • Tamil Sangam Literature: Earliest regional literature (1st–3rd century CE)
  • Texts: Tolkappiyam (grammar), Silappadikaram, Manimekalai
  • Theme: Love (Akam) and War (Puram)

8. Scientific Texts as Literature

  • Charaka Samhita: Ancient text on Ayurveda
  • Sushruta Samhita: Surgery and medical procedures
  • Manusmriti: Law book & social ethics text

PART 3: Science, Mathematics & Astronomy

"India measured the sky and split the atom, while the world still measured time by shadows." — Bibliotech

1. Mathematics

  • Concept of Zero: Invented in India — first recorded use by Brahmagupta (7th century)
  • Decimal System: Developed in India and transmitted to Arabs, then Europe
  • Aryabhata:
    • Author of Aryabhatiya (499 CE)
    • Introduced place value system
    • Calculated π (pi) ≈ 3.1416
  • Brahmagupta:
    • Work: Brahmasphutasiddhanta
    • Rules for zero, negative numbers, quadratic equations
  • Bhaskara I and II:
    • Advancements in algebra, trigonometry, and calculus
    • Siddhanta Shiromani – major mathematical treatise

2. Astronomy

  • Aryabhata: Proposed that earth rotates on its axis
  • Explained solar and lunar eclipses scientifically
  • Calculated length of solar year (~365.358 days)
  • Varahamihira:
    • Wrote Pancha Siddhantika – summary of five astronomical systems
    • Brihatsamhita: encyclopedia of astronomy, astrology, weather
  • Lagadha: Wrote Vedanga Jyotisha, one of the earliest texts on astronomy

3. Medicine & Surgery

  • Charaka Samhita: Father of Indian medicine (Charaka) — emphasis on health, diet, diagnosis
  • Sushruta Samhita: Described 300+ surgical procedures, 120+ instruments
  • Plastic surgery, cataract removal, stitching wounds — with detailed procedures
  • Ayurveda developed as a complete system of medicine — tridosha theory (Vata, Pitta, Kapha)

4. Metallurgy & Engineering

  • Iron Pillar of Mehrauli (Delhi): Rust-free pillar from Gupta period — metallurgical marvel
  • Texts like Rasaratnakara mention gold, silver, mercury processing
  • Smelting, coin minting, urban drainage — all show technical mastery

5. Environmental Awareness

  • Vedic texts praise natural elements: rivers, sun, wind, fire
  • Arthashastra recommends conservation of forests and wildlife
  • Buddhist-Jain texts emphasize non-violence and harmony with nature
