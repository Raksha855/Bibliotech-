DPSP vs Fundamental Rights

The Indian Constitution maintains a fine balance between Fundamental Rights (Part III) and Directive Principles (Part IV). While FRs are justiciable and enforceable in courts, DPSPs are non-justiciable but act as moral & constitutional guidance for governance.

Key Differences

Aspect Fundamental Rights Directive Principles
Nature Justiciable (enforceable in court) Non-justiciable (not enforceable in court)
Aim Political Democracy Social and Economic Democracy
Source USA’s Bill of Rights Irish Constitution
Focus Individual rights Welfare of society
Enforcement By courts (Article 32, 226) No legal enforcement, only moral obligation

Judicial View: Important Cases

  • Champakam Dorairajan Case (1951): FRs > DPSPs. Parliament cannot override FRs for implementing DPSPs.
  • Kesavananda Bharati Case (1973): Balance must be maintained. Both are part of Basic Structure.
  • Minerva Mills Case (1980): "Harmony & balance between FRs and DPSPs is essential" — Both are complementary.

Amendments that gave priority to DPSPs

  • 42nd Amendment (1976): Gave precedence to DPSPs over FRs in Article 31C for implementing socialist goals.
  • 44th Amendment (1978): Limited Article 31C only to Article 39(b) and 39(c).

Conclusion

DPSPs and FRs are not contradictory but complementary. One ensures freedom, the other ensures justice and equality. Together, they make India a truly welfare-oriented democratic republic.
