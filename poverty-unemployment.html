Poverty & Unemployment

"True development begins when poverty ends and opportunity begins." — Bibliotech

What is Poverty?

Poverty is a condition where individuals are unable to meet the basic necessities of life — food, clothing, shelter, health, and education.

Types of Poverty

  • Absolute Poverty: Income below minimum level needed for basic subsistence
  • Relative Poverty: Income less than average standard in society
  • Urban vs Rural Poverty: Location-based poverty characteristics
  • Chronic Poverty: Long-term, intergenerational poverty

Poverty Indicators

  • Head Count Ratio (HCR)
  • Multidimensional Poverty Index (MPI)
  • Below Poverty Line (BPL) data
  • Tendulkar & Rangarajan Committees

Major Poverty Alleviation Programs

  • MGNREGA – 100 days of wage employment
  • PM Awas Yojana – Affordable housing
  • NSAP – Pension for vulnerable groups
  • National Food Security Act (NFSA)

What is Unemployment?

Unemployment refers to a situation where people who are willing and able to work cannot find jobs.

Types of Unemployment in India

Type Description
Structural Mismatch between skills and job requirements
Frictional Temporary unemployment during job change
Seasonal Jobs available only in certain seasons (e.g. agriculture)
Disguised More people employed than required — marginal productivity is zero
Educated Unemployment Qualified individuals unable to find suitable jobs

Unemployment Measurement

  • Usual Status (1 year)
  • Current Weekly Status (CWS)
  • Current Daily Status (CDS)
  • Data Source: Periodic Labour Force Survey (PLFS)

Government Schemes for Employment

  • Skill India Mission
  • Start-Up India
  • Stand-Up India
  • PMEGP – Employment through enterprise
  • National Career Service Portal
