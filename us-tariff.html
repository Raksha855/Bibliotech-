US Hikes Tariff on Indian Imports to 26%

“Trade war may be silent, but its impact echoes across economies.” — Bibliotech

What Happened?

In April 2025, the United States announced a tariff hike on select Indian imports, primarily textiles, aluminum, pharma ingredients, and steel, raising duties up to 26%. This move is part of a broader trade recalibration by the US under its ‘Protect American Industries’ strategy.

Why Did It Happen?

  • Concerns over trade imbalances and rising Indian exports to the US market
  • US domestic lobbies pressurizing for protectionism amid elections
  • Alleged undercutting in price by Indian manufacturers
  • US-India disputes at WTO unresolved over certain pharma and steel subsidies

Impact on India

  • Indian exports worth over $8 billion could face losses
  • SMEs (Small and Medium Enterprises) in textiles and pharma hit the hardest
  • May slow down India’s ambition to increase merchandise exports to $1 trillion by 2030
  • Pressure on bilateral ties and delay in the India-US trade pact

India's Response

  • Commerce Ministry has termed the hike as “discriminatory”
  • India is exploring reciprocal tariffs or raising the issue at the WTO
  • Negotiation channels opened to seek a rollback on pharma and aluminum duties

US-India Trade Tensions: A Deeper Insight

"Trade decisions reflect more than just economics — they are the new tools of diplomacy." — Bibliotech

India's WTO Strategy

India is preparing to formally raise the tariff issue in the WTO Dispute Settlement Body. Citing violation of non-discrimination principles under GATT, the government is working closely with trade allies in the Global South to build a consensus.

Retaliatory Measures Being Considered

  • Tariff increases on select US agro-products and electric vehicles
  • Restrictions on US tech giants over data localization & privacy violations
  • Expanding trade deals with ASEAN and EU as counterbalance

Impact on India’s Trade Goals

The tariff hike could slow India's progress toward its ambitious goal of achieving $1 trillion in exports by 2030. Sectors like engineering goods, garments, and chemicals are under pressure to diversify markets.

Strategic Recalibration

India is reviewing its trade dependency on any single country and reviving talks on Free Trade Agreements (FTAs) with EU, UK, and Canada. Experts believe this is a moment for India to push for self-reliant manufacturing and trusted value chains.

Global Reactions

  • WTO: Expressed concern over rising protectionism in US and called for multilateral cooperation
  • EU: Warned US actions could trigger reciprocal retaliations globally
  • Japan, Australia: Support India’s stand for fair market access
