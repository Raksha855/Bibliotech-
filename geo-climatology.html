Climatology

"Climate is what we expect, weather is what we get." — Mark Twain

What is Climatology?

Climatology is the scientific study of climate, atmospheric patterns, and long-term weather conditions. It helps us understand natural disasters, agriculture, monsoon behavior, and climate change.

Structure of the Atmosphere

Layer Height Feature
Troposphere 0–12 km Weather phenomena, temperature decreases with height
Stratosphere 12–50 km Ozone layer, temperature increases with height
Mesosphere 50–80 km Meteors burn here
Thermosphere 80–500 km Auroras, satellite orbits
Exosphere 500+ km Outer space begins

Heat Zones of the Earth

  • Torrid Zone (between Tropics)
  • Temperate Zones (between Tropics and Circles)
  • Frigid Zones (near poles)

Pressure Belts

  • Equatorial Low Pressure Belt
  • Subtropical High Pressure Belt
  • Subpolar Low Pressure Belt
  • Polar High Pressure Belt

Winds

  • Permanent Winds: Trade Winds, Westerlies, Polar Easterlies
  • Seasonal Winds: Monsoons
  • Local Winds: Loo, Chinook, Mistral, Foehn

Rainfall Types

  • Convectional Rainfall: Due to rising warm air
  • Orographic Rainfall: Due to mountains (e.g., Western Ghats)
  • Cyclonic Rainfall: Due to low-pressure systems

El Niño, La Niña & ENSO

  • El Niño: Warming of Pacific Ocean, weakens Indian monsoon
  • La Niña: Opposite of El Niño, stronger monsoon
  • ENSO: El Niño + Southern Oscillation (atmospheric pressure changes)

Indian Monsoon

  • Caused by differential heating of land & ocean
  • Arabian Sea & Bay of Bengal branches
  • Breaks and bursts of monsoon important for UPSC

UPSC Relevance

  • Prelims: Atmosphere layers, winds, rainfall types
  • Mains GS1: Indian monsoon, climate change, weather extremes
  • Essay: “Climate is the unacknowledged dictator of civilization”
