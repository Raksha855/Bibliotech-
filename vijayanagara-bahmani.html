Vijayanagara & Bahmani Sultanates

"Two mighty powers of the Deccan — divided by faith, united by ambition." — Bibliotech

Foundations

  • Vijayanagara Empire (1336–1646): Founded by **Harihara I and Bukka I** of the Sangama dynasty under the influence of sage Vidyaranya.
  • Bahmani Sultanate (1347–1527): Founded by **Hasan Gangu** (Ala-ud-din Bahman Shah) after revolt against Delhi Sultanate.

Capital Cities

  • Vijayanagara: Hampi — on the banks of Tungabhadra River; fortified & planned city with Dravidian architecture.
  • Bahmani: Gulbarga (Ahsanabad) and later **Bidar** as the capital.

Administration & Economy

  • Amara-Nayaka System: Military chiefs received land revenue in return for maintaining troops (like Iqta).
  • Gold coin called **Pagoda** used in Vijayanagara; Bahmanis minted **fals, tankas**.
  • Flourishing agriculture via irrigation tanks, canals; trade with Arabs, Portuguese.

Art & Culture

  • Vijayanagara: Dravidian temples, gopurams, Hampi’s Virupaksha Temple, stone chariots.
  • Bahmani: Persian-Islamic architecture, Bidar fort, Madarsa of Mahmud Gawan.
  • Krishna Deva Raya — poet & patron, wrote *Amuktamalyada* in Telugu.

Religious Life

  • Vijayanagara kings patronized **Shaivism, Vaishnavism**, and **Advaita philosophy**.
  • Bahmani rulers initially Sunni Muslims; later phases saw Persian cultural influence.

Conflict & Decline

  • Frequent wars between Vijayanagara and Bahmani over fertile **Raichur Doab** region.
  • Bahmani declined into **5 Deccan Sultanates**: Bijapur, Golconda, Ahmadnagar, Bidar, Berar.
  • Vijayanagara collapsed after **Battle of Talikota (1565)** against combined Deccan Sultanates.
