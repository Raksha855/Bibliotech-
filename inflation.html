Inflation

“Inflation is taxation without legislation.” — Milton Friedman

What is Inflation?

Inflation is the rate at which the general level of prices for goods and services rises, eroding the purchasing power of money. It is measured as an annual percentage change.

Causes of Inflation

  • Demand-Pull Inflation: Caused by an increase in aggregate demand
  • Cost-Push Inflation: Rising production costs push prices up
  • Imported Inflation: Due to rising prices of imports (like crude oil)
  • Monetary Inflation: Excess money supply in the economy

Types of Inflation

Type Description
Moderate Inflation Slow rise in prices (manageable)
Galloping Inflation Rapid and uncontrolled rise in prices
Hyperinflation Extremely high, typically 100%+ monthly inflation
Stagflation Inflation + unemployment + stagnant growth
Disinflation Decrease in the rate of inflation (still rising prices, but slower)
Deflation Fall in the general price level of goods & services

Measurement of Inflation in India

  • CPI (Consumer Price Index): Reflects retail inflation — RBI uses this for policy decisions
  • WPI (Wholesale Price Index): Measures inflation at wholesale level

Impact of Inflation

  • Reduces purchasing power of consumers
  • Affects savings and investments
  • Creates uncertainty in economy
  • Encourages hoarding and black marketing

Inflation Control Measures

  • Monetary Policy: RBI uses repo rate, CRR, SLR, etc.
  • Fiscal Policy: Reducing fiscal deficit and rationalizing subsidies
  • Supply-side Policies: Improving production & distribution efficiency

UPSC Relevance

  • Very important in GS Paper 3 (Indian Economy)
  • Also appears in Prelims (CPI, WPI, repo rate, etc.)
  • Use inflation-related data in essays & ethics case studies
