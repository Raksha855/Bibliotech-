DPSP – Article-wise Summary (Articles 36 to 51)

Let’s explore all Directive Principles of State Policy from Article 36 to 51, along with classification and examples.

Article 36:

Defines “State” — same meaning as in Article 12 of Fundamental Rights.

Article 37:

DPSPs are non-justiciable but fundamental in governance.

Socialist Principles

  • Article 38: State to secure a social order for promotion of welfare.
  • Article 39: Equal justice, livelihood, equal pay, health of workers, children.
  • Article 39A: Free legal aid and equal justice (added by 42nd Amendment).
  • Article 41: Right to work, education and public assistance in certain cases.
  • Article 42: Just and humane conditions of work and maternity relief.
  • Article 43: Living wage, decent standard of life for all workers.
  • Article 43A: Workers’ participation in management (added by 42nd Amendment).
  • Article 47: Duty of state to raise nutrition and standard of living.

Gandhian Principles

  • Article 40: Organization of Village Panchayats.
  • Article 43: Promotion of cottage industries.
  • Article 46: Promotion of educational and economic interests of SCs, STs and other weaker sections.
  • Article 47: Prohibition of intoxicating drinks and drugs.
  • Article 48: Organization of agriculture and animal husbandry (includes ban on cow slaughter).

Liberal-Intellectual Principles

  • Article 44: Uniform Civil Code for the citizens.
  • Article 45: Free and compulsory education for children under age 14. *(Now part of Article 21A)*
  • Article 48: Protection and improvement of environment and safeguarding of forests and wildlife.
  • Article 49: Protection of monuments of national importance.
  • Article 50: Separation of judiciary from executive.
  • Article 51: Promotion of international peace and security.

Examples in Action

  • Mid-Day Meal Scheme: Based on Article 47 (nutrition)
  • Legal Services Authority Act: Based on Article 39A
  • Panchayati Raj Acts: Based on Article 40
