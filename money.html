Money & Its Types

"Money is a matter of functions four: a medium, a measure, a standard, and a store." — William Stanley Jevons

What is Money?

Money is anything that is generally accepted as a medium of exchange, a unit of account, a store of value, and a standard of deferred payment. It replaced the barter system and resolved the issue of double coincidence of wants.

Functions of Money

  • Medium of Exchange: Facilitates buying & selling without barter
  • Unit of Account: Standard measure of value for goods & services
  • Store of Value: Can be saved and used in future
  • Standard of Deferred Payment: Used for future payments/credit

Types of Money

Type Description Examples
Commodity Money Money with intrinsic value Gold, Silver, Salt
Fiat Money Declared legal tender by the government ₹500 note, ₹10 coin
Fiduciary Money Accepted on the basis of trust Cheques, Promissory notes
Bank Money Money in digital/deposit form Savings account, UPI
CBDC Digital currency issued by Central Bank Digital Rupee (by RBI)
Cryptocurrency Decentralized digital currency Bitcoin, Ethereum
