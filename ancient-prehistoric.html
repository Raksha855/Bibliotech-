Prehistoric Period of India

"Before scripts, before empires, there were caves, tools, and fire — and that's where our story begins." — Bibliotech

What is Prehistory?

Prehistory refers to the time **before the invention of writing**. In India, this era is understood only through **archaeological evidence** like tools, cave paintings, and bones.

Chronological Division of Prehistoric Period

  • Paleolithic Age (Old Stone Age) – 500,000 BCE to 10,000 BCE
  • Mesolithic Age (Middle Stone Age) – 10,000 BCE to 8000 BCE
  • Neolithic Age (New Stone Age) – 8000 BCE to 1000 BCE

1. Paleolithic Age

  • People were hunter-gatherers and lived in caves.
  • Tools: crude stone tools, hand axes, cleavers
  • Major Sites: **Sohan Valley**, **Hunsgi**, **Attirampakkam**, **Bhimbetka**
  • Important Discovery: **Bhimbetka cave paintings** in Madhya Pradesh

2. Mesolithic Age

  • Transition from hunting to food gathering and beginning of domestication
  • Microlith tools used (tiny tools with better edges)
  • Evidence of **semi-permanent houses** and **early burial practices**
  • Sites: Bagor (Rajasthan), Sarai Nahar Rai (UP)

3. Neolithic Age

  • Beginning of agriculture & animal domestication
  • Tools became polished and more specialized
  • People started living in **mud houses**
  • Pottery began to appear
  • Sites: Burzahom (Kashmir), Chirand (Bihar), Hallur (Karnataka), Mehrgarh (Pakistan)

Important Note:

India saw the **beginning of a settled life** during Neolithic, laying the foundation for **civilizations like Indus Valley**.
