Art & Architecture in Medieval India

"From domes that touched the sky to minarets whispering history — medieval India carved stories in stone." — Bibliotech

Introduction

Medieval Indian art and architecture reflects the **fusion of Indo-Islamic styles**, with bold experiments in domes, arches, minarets, forts, tombs, and gardens. Both **Delhi Sultanate** and **Mughal Empire** left behind breathtaking monuments that tell tales of power, culture, and aesthetics.

Delhi Sultanate Architecture (1206–1526)

  • Influence: Turkish-Persian styles merged with Indian stone carving traditions.
  • Key Features: Arches, domes, pointed minarets, Quranic calligraphy, use of red sandstone.
  • Builders: Slave, Khilji, Tughlaq, Sayyid, and Lodi dynasties.

Major Monuments

  • Qutub Minar: Started by Qutb-ud-din Aibak, completed by Iltutmish. World's tallest brick minaret.
  • Quwwat-ul-Islam Mosque: First mosque of India built with reused Hindu temple material.
  • Alai Darwaza: By Alauddin Khilji – dome, horseshoe arch, decorative inscriptions.
  • Tughlaqabad Fort: Massive fort with sloping stone walls, built by Ghiyas-ud-din Tughlaq.
  • Lodi Gardens: Tombs of Sikandar and Muhammad Lodi — double domes and octagonal chambers.

Mughal Architecture (1526–1857)

  • Influence: Persian + Central Asian + Indian styles = Grand Indo-Islamic architecture.
  • Key Features: Bulbous domes, symmetry, Charbagh gardens, pietra dura (marble inlay), double domes, arches.
  • Focus: Aesthetics, symmetry, spirituality, imperial grandeur.

Major Emperors and Their Contributions

Emperor Monuments
Babur Laid Charbagh gardens in Agra; introduced Timurid styles.
Humayun Humayun’s Tomb in Delhi — precursor to Taj Mahal, Persian influence.
Akbar Fatehpur Sikri, Agra Fort, Buland Darwaza, Tomb of Sheikh Salim Chishti — blend of Hindu, Jain, Islamic styles.
Jahangir Shalimar Gardens (Kashmir), emphasis on painting & floral motifs.
Shah Jahan Taj Mahal (Agra), Red Fort (Delhi), Jama Masjid, Moti Masjid — pinnacle of Mughal architecture.
Aurangzeb Modest architecture; Bibi Ka Maqbara (Aurangabad) built in memory of wife.

Painting & Decorative Art

  • Miniature Paintings — flourished under Mughals, especially Jahangir.
  • Persian influence with Indian themes: flora, fauna, court scenes, portraits.
  • Workshops (Karkhanas) developed for royal patronage of artists.
