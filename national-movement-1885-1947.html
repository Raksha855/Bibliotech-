Indian National Movement (1885-1947)

“The national movement is not a moment in history, but a movement of a civilization reclaiming its soul.”
Phase 1: Moderate Phase (1885–1905)

Leaders:

  • Dadabhai Naoroji
  • Gopal Krishna Gokhale
  • Pherozeshah Mehta

Features:

  • Faith in British justice
  • Petitioning & constitutional reforms
  • Economic critique of colonialism (Drain of Wealth Theory – Dadabhai Naoroji)

Limitations:

  • Limited mass participation
  • Over-dependence on British goodwill
Phase 2: Extremist Phase (1905–1917)

Leaders:

  • Bal Gangadhar Tilak
  • Lala Lajpat Rai
  • Bipin Chandra Pal

Key Events:

  • Partition of Bengal (1905)
  • Swadeshi & Boycott Movement (1905–08)
  • Formation of Muslim League (1906)

Slogan:

  • “Swaraj is my birthright and I shall have it.” — Tilak
Phase 3: Home Rule Movement & WWI (1916–1919)

Key Developments:

  • Home Rule League by Tilak and Annie Besant (1916)
  • Lucknow Pact (1916) – INC & Muslim League unity
Phase 4: Gandhian Era (1919–1947)

1. Non-Cooperation Movement (1920–22)

  • Jallianwala Bagh Massacre (1919)
  • Khilafat Movement
  • Boycott of schools, courts, British goods
  • Withdrawn after Chauri Chaura incident

2. Civil Disobedience Movement (1930–34)

  • Salt March (Dandi Yatra)
  • Complete non-payment of taxes
  • First Round Table Conference boycott
  • Gandhi-Irwin Pact (1931)

3. Quit India Movement (1942)

  • “Do or Die” slogan
  • Mass arrests, violence, underground activity
  • Women and students' active role
Other Important Developments

Revolutionary Movements:

  • Bhagat Singh, Chandrashekhar Azad, Hindustan Socialist Republican Association (HSRA)
  • Kakori Conspiracy, Lahore Conspiracy

Subhas Chandra Bose & INA:

  • Forward Bloc formation
  • Azad Hind Fauj (INA) – “Give me blood and I will give you freedom”

Labour & Kisan Movements:

  • Trade unions, Kisan Sabha (1936)
  • Role in mobilizing masses against colonial rule

British Response:

  • Rowlatt Act (1919)
  • Government of India Acts (1919 & 1935)
  • Cripps Mission (1942), Cabinet Mission (1946)
End of British Rule (1947)
  • Mountbatten Plan
  • Indian Independence Act (July 1947)
  • Partition of India — Pakistan created
  • India became Independent on 15th August 1947
“At the stroke of the midnight hour, when the world sleeps, India will awake to life and freedom.” — Pt. Jawaharlal Nehru

Additional Insights: Indian National Movement (1885–1947)

Did you know? The Indian National Movement was not just political; it encompassed social, economic, and cultural dimensions that played a crucial role in uniting the nation.

1. Role of Women in the Freedom Struggle

  • Sarojini Naidu: Also known as the 'Nightingale of India', she was a prominent leader and poet who played a vital role in the Civil Disobedience Movement.
  • Aruna Asaf Ali: Hoisted the Indian National Congress flag during the Quit India Movement in 1942.
  • Kasturba Gandhi: Actively participated in the Non-Cooperation Movement and was a pillar of support for Mahatma Gandhi.

2. Cultural Renaissance and Nationalism

  • Rabindranath Tagore: His literary works instilled a sense of pride and nationalism among Indians.
  • Bankim Chandra Chattopadhyay: Composed 'Vande Mataram', which became a source of inspiration for freedom fighters.
  • Swami Vivekananda: Emphasized the importance of spiritual awakening and national pride.

3. Economic Impact of British Policies

  • Deindustrialization: Traditional industries suffered due to British imports and policies.
  • Land Revenue Systems: Systems like Zamindari led to exploitation of peasants.
  • Drain of Wealth: Concept highlighted by Dadabhai Naoroji, emphasizing the economic exploitation of India.

4. Revolutionary Movements

  • Bhagat Singh: Advocated for complete independence and was involved in the Lahore Conspiracy Case.
  • Chandrashekhar Azad: Key figure in the Hindustan Socialist Republican Association (HSRA).
  • Subhas Chandra Bose: Formed the Indian National Army (INA) to fight against British rule.

5. International Influence and Support

  • World Wars: India's involvement in World Wars highlighted the need for self-governance.
  • Global Leaders: Figures like Lenin and movements like the Irish struggle inspired Indian revolutionaries.
