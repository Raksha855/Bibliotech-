Parliament vs State Legislature

“The Parliament of India protects national interests; the State Legislature preserves the soul of federalism.” — Bibliotech
Feature / Basis Parliament (Union) State Legislature
Articles Article 79 to 122 Article 168 to 212
Structure President + Lok Sabha + Rajya Sabha Governor + Vidhan Sabha (+ Parishad if bicameral)
Head of House Speaker (LS), Vice-President (RS) Speaker (VS), Chairman (VP)
Max Strength LS – 552, RS – 250 VS – 500 (max), Parishad – 1/3rd of VS
Tenure LS – 5 years; RS – permanent VS – 5 years; Parishad – permanent
Method of Election LS – Direct; RS – Indirect VS – Direct; Parishad – Indirect/Nominated
Legislative Powers Union + Concurrent Lists State + Concurrent Lists
Money Bill Only in Lok Sabha Only in Vidhan Sabha
Budget Union Budget State Budget
Dissolution Lok Sabha by President Vidhan Sabha by Governor
Special Powers Amend Constitution, All India Services Limited to state subjects
Judicial Role Impeachment, Judicial removal None
Privileges Article 105 Article 194
Anti-Defection Applicable (Tenth Schedule) Applicable (Tenth Schedule)
