Economic Growth & Development

"Growth is about quantity, development is about quality of life." — Bibliotech

What is Economic Growth?

Economic growth refers to the increase in the output of goods and services in an economy over a period of time. It is usually measured as a percentage increase in real GDP.

What is Economic Development?

Economic development is a broader concept that includes economic growth plus improvements in living standards, education, health, equality, and freedom.

Key Differences

Aspect Economic Growth Economic Development
Focus Increase in output (GDP) Overall improvement in quality of life
Measurement GDP, GNP HDI, MPI, Gini Index
Nature Quantitative Qualitative + Quantitative
Time Frame Short or medium term Long-term transformation

Important Indicators

  • HDI (Human Development Index): Life expectancy, education, and per capita income
  • Gini Coefficient: Measures inequality (0 = perfect equality)
  • GDP Growth Rate: Real % change in GDP
  • MPI (Multidimensional Poverty Index): Poverty beyond income (health, education, living standards)

Inclusive Growth

Growth that is broad-based, reduces inequality, generates employment and is sustainable. This is a key goal of India’s development policies and UPSC GS Paper 3.

UPSC Relevance

  • Prelims: HDI, GNP vs GDP, Gini Index
  • Mains: GS Paper 3 topics like poverty, inequality, growth models
  • Essay: “Growth without development is hollow” or “India’s tryst with inclusive development”
