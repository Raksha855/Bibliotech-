Post-Gupta Period (650–1200 CE)

"Empires faded but culture endured — the post-Gupta age laid India’s medieval foundations." — Bibliotech

Background

After the decline of the Gupta Empire, India entered into a period of political decentralization and regional power struggles. However, art, literature, and education continued to flourish.

1. Pala Dynasty (Eastern India)

  • Founded by Gopala (elected by people)
  • Dharmapala and Devapala expanded the empire
  • Patrons of Mahavihara at Nalanda & Vikramshila
  • Promoted Mahayana Buddhism

2. Pratihara Dynasty (Western India)

  • Founder: Nagabhata I
  • Most famous ruler: Raja Bhoja
  • Acted as a barrier to Arab invasions (from Sindh)
  • Capital: Kannauj
  • Patrons of Sanskrit literature and temple art

3. Rashtrakuta Dynasty (Deccan)

  • Founder: Dantidurga
  • Greatest ruler: Amoghavarsha (scholar, poet)
  • Capital: Malkhed (Manyakheta)
  • Built the famous Kailasa Temple at Ellora
  • Patrons of Jainism and Sanskrit & Kannada literature

Tripartite Struggle

  • Palas, Pratiharas, and Rashtrakutas fought over control of Kannauj
  • This rivalry lasted for almost 200 years
  • Weakened all three empires eventually

Culture & Education

  • Universities: Nalanda, Vikramshila, Odantapuri flourished under Palas
  • Temple architecture: Nagara (North), Dravida (South), Vesara (Deccan)
  • Growth of regional languages: Kannada, Tamil, Bengali

UPSC Relevance

  • Prelims: Dynasties, locations, art styles, temples
  • Mains GS1: Political and cultural transition from ancient to medieval
  • Essay: “Tripartite India: Power and culture in regional hands”
