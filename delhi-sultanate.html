Delhi Sultanate (1206–1526)

“The Delhi Sultanate laid the foundation of Indo‑Islamic polity and culture.” — Bibliotech

Dynastic Overview

1. Administration & Revenue

  • Iqta System: Grant of revenue assignments to officers in lieu of salary.
  • Diwan‑i‑Wazarat: Finance department overseeing revenue collection.
  • Land Revenue: Measured in dams, silver coinage; zabt and misr systems under Alauddin.
  • Provincial Governance: Governed by governors (muqtis, amirs) under Sultan’s authority.

2. Society & Economy

  • Growth of market towns (saras, havelis); rise of a new urban artisan class.
  • Introduction of Turkic‑Iranian nobility; gradual assimilation with Indian elites.
  • Trade routes: Overland via Silk Route, maritime through Calicut & Cambay.
  • Introduction of new crops (millets, sugar‑cane), improved irrigation (tanks, wells).

3. Culture, Art & Architecture

  • Qutb Complex: Qutb Minar, Alai Darwaza, Quwwat ul‑Islam Mosque.
  • Tomb Architecture: Iltutmish’s tomb, Hauz Khas Complex; early use of arches and domes.
  • Indo‑Islamic Fusion: Use of red sandstone, intricate calligraphy, Persian gardens (charbagh conceptual precursor).
  • Patronage of Persian language; Sufi khanqahs and Urdu’s antecedents in Hindavi.

4. Military & Technology

  • Use of cavalry and specialist mounted archers (Turkic tradition).
  • Fortifications: Siri, Tughlaqabad, Firozabad forts; strategic hill forts in Rajasthan.
  • Introduction of gunpowder weapons under later Tughlaqs.

5. Religious & Social Movements

  • Growth of Sufism: Chishti, Suhrawardi, Qadiri orders; emphasis on syncretic ethos.
  • Bhakti movements (Kabir, Nanak) critiquing ritualism, promoting devotional unity.
  • Religious tolerance fluctuated—Raziya’s patronage vs. Firuz Shah’s religious endowments.

6. Decline & Legacy

  • Weakening central authority under Late Tughlaqs and Sayyids; rise of regional sultanates (Bengal, Malwa).
  • Internal revolts, economic pressures, Mongol invasions, and Lodi‑Sher Shah succession struggles.
  • Laid groundwork for Mughal polity, administration (Diwan, mansabdari), and composite culture.

UPSC Relevance

  • Prelims: Key rulers, Qutb Minar, Iqta system, Alauddin’s market reforms.
  • Mains: Administrative evolution, agrarian changes, architectural innovations.
  • Essay/GS4: Syncretic culture, Sufi‑Bhakti interaction, legacy of Delhi Sultanate.
