"When kingdoms rose and kings ruled — two great thinkers chose peace over power." — Bibliotech

1. Rise of Mahajanapadas (600 BCE)

After the decline of the Vedic tribes, the Indian subcontinent saw the emergence of **16 Mahajanapadas**, which were large kingdoms and republics. This marked the beginning of organized political units.

Major Sources:

  • Buddhist text: Anguttara Nikaya
  • Jain text: Bhagavati Sutra

Classification:

  • Monarchies: Magadha, Kashi, Kosala, Vatsa
  • Republics (Ganarajyas): Vajji, Malla, Sakyas

Most Powerful Mahajanapadas:

  • Magadha: Most dominant (rulers: Bimbisara, Ajatashatru)
  • Vajji: Strong confederacy of republics (capital: Vaishali)
  • Kosala: Birthplace of Lord Rama, ruled by Prasenjit
  • Avanti: Western power, capital at Ujjain

Reasons for Magadha’s Rise:

  • Fertile Gangetic plains
  • Rich iron deposits in Bihar
  • Strong rulers like Bimbisara & Ajatashatru
  • Strategic capital at Rajgir & later Pataliputra

Map-Based Question Tip (UPSC):

Practice locating 16 Mahajanapadas on a map — especially Magadha, Kosala, Avanti, Vajji.

UPSC Relevance:

  • Prelims: Location-based MCQs, texts, kings
  • Mains GS1: Evolution of polity in India
  • Essay: "From republics to empires: India’s political journey"

2. Jainism: The Path of Renunciation

"Conquer yourself and you conquer the world — said the Tirthankaras." — Bibliotech

Founder and Background

  • Jainism was founded by Rishabhanatha (1st Tirthankara)
  • Mahavira (24th Tirthankara) is regarded as the real founder (born in 540 BCE)
  • Born in a Kshatriya family in Kundagrama (Vaishali), contemporary of Buddha
  • Attained Kevalya (omniscience) under a sal tree at 42

Key Teachings of Jainism

  • Five Vows (Mahavratas):
    • Ahimsa (non-violence)
    • Satya (truth)
    • Asteya (non-stealing)
    • Brahmacharya (celibacy)
    • Aparigraha (non-possession)
  • Karma theory: Karma binds the soul (Jiva), causes rebirth
  • Moksha through purification, not through rituals
  • Rejects Vedas, rituals, caste system

Jain Texts

  • Agamas: Sacred Jain texts written in Prakrit
  • Compiled at Vallabhi (Gujarat) in the 6th century CE

Sects of Jainism

  • Svetambaras: Wear white clothes, allow women monks, live in community
  • Digambaras: Practice nudity, stricter, don’t believe women can attain moksha

Symbols & Legacy

  • Jain symbol: Ahimsa hand
  • Famous sites: Shravanabelagola, Palitana, Mt. Abu

UPSC Relevance:

  • Prelims: Tirthankaras, symbols, texts, locations
  • Mains GS1: Evolution of religious thought
  • Ethics Paper: Ahimsa, Aparigraha principles

3. Buddhism: The Middle Path

"The mind is everything. What you think you become." — Gautam Buddha

Life of Gautam Buddha

  • Born as Siddhartha in Lumbini (563 BCE), Kshatriya of Shakya clan
  • Four sights changed his life: old age, sickness, death, ascetic
  • Left palace at 29 ("Mahabhinishkramana")
  • Attained enlightenment at Bodh Gaya under Bodhi tree (age 35)
  • Gave first sermon at Sarnath ("Dharmachakra Pravartana")
  • Died at Kushinagar (age 80, "Mahaparinirvana")

Core Teachings of Buddhism

  • Four Noble Truths:
    • Dukkha – Suffering exists
    • Samudaya – There is a cause of suffering
    • Nirodha – Suffering can end
    • Magga – The path to end suffering
  • Eightfold Path: Right view, thought, speech, action, livelihood, effort, mindfulness, concentration
  • Middle Path: Avoid extremes of indulgence and austerity

Buddhist Councils

Council Place Ruler Outcome
1st Rajgir Ajatashatru Compilation of Sutta & Vinaya Pitaka
2nd Vaishali Kalashoka First schism in Sangha
3rd Pataliputra Ashoka Spread to Sri Lanka; Abhidhamma Pitaka added
4th Kundalvana (Kashmir) Kanishka Division into Mahayana & Hinayana

Sects of Buddhism

  • Hinayana: Conservative, individual salvation, Pali texts
  • Mahayana: Liberal, idol worship, Sanskrit texts, Bodhisattvas
  • Vajrayana: Emerged in Tibet, magical rituals

Buddhist Art & Legacy

  • Stupas: Sanchi, Amaravati
  • Chaityas and Viharas
  • Nalanda & Vikramshila universities

Decline of Buddhism in India

  • Loss of patronage, internal corruption
  • Revival of Hinduism (Bhakti movement)
  • Destruction of monasteries by invaders

UPSC Relevance:

  • Prelims: Councils, places, teachings
  • Mains: GS1 – Religious development in India
  • Ethics: Buddhist philosophy of mindfulness and middle path

Mauryan Empire

"From the sword of Chandragupta to the dhamma of Ashoka — India witnessed her first great empire." — Bibliotech

Introduction

The Mauryan Empire (322–185 BCE) was the first large-scale empire in Indian history. Founded by Chandragupta Maurya with the guidance of Chanakya (Kautilya), the empire reached its zenith under Ashoka the Great.
