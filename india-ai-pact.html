India Signs Global AI Ethics Pact at the United Nations

“AI can create wonders — only if guided by wisdom and ethics.” — Bibliotech

What Happened?

At the UN's International Digital Governance Summit held in Geneva in March 2025, India signed a historic Global AI Ethics Pact along with 40 other nations. This multilateral agreement aims to promote responsible, ethical, and inclusive development of Artificial Intelligence (AI).

Key Principles of the Pact

  • Transparency and Explainability in AI Systems
  • Bias-Free and Non-Discriminatory Algorithms
  • Respect for Human Rights and Privacy
  • Global Collaboration on AI Research and Safety
  • Protection against misuse of AI in surveillance & warfare

India’s Stand on AI Ethics

  • India emphasized “AI for All” — inclusive and equitable access
  • Proposal to create a UN-led AI Oversight Council
  • Support for Open Source AI and transparency in Big Tech
  • Focus on using AI for agriculture, education, and health

Why It Matters

  • AI is shaping governance, defense, jobs, education, and media
  • Lack of global standards can lead to exploitation, surveillance abuse, and digital divide
  • India positioning itself as a moral tech leader globally

Comparison: Ethical AI Commitments Globally

  • EU: Introduced AI Act to regulate high-risk systems
  • US: National AI Initiative Act passed in 2020; still lacks federal ethics law
  • India: Proposing ethical-by-design model via NITI Aayog & MeitY

  • GS3: Science & Tech – Ethical Concerns of AI, Cybersecurity

India’s AI Ethics Pact at UN: Extended Insights

“Ethics is not a constraint on technology; it is its compass.” — Bibliotech

1. Global Conference on AI, Security & Ethics 2025

Held in Geneva under UNIDIR’s banner, the inaugural AI Security & Ethics Summit (#AISE25) gathered diplomats, industry leaders, academics, and civil society to craft a unified approach toward AI governance. India co‑chaired key panels on “Inclusive AI for Development” and “AI in National Security”.

2. Why US & UK Did Not Sign

While 40 nations endorsed the pact, the United States and United Kingdom abstained. Their concerns centered on:

  • National Security Exceptions: Fear that strict ethics rules could hamper defense R&D.
  • Regulatory Burden: Aversion to binding international norms over domestic innovation policies.
  • Sovereignty: Desire to retain unilateral control over AI standards and data flows.

3. India’s Broader AI Regulation Landscape

A recent Carnegie Endowment paper (Nov 2024) outlines India’s tripartite AI policy roadmap:

  • Legislative Framework: Draft AI Act focusing on risk‑based classification.
  • Standards & Certification: Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS) to define AI quality benchmarks.
  • Multi‑stakeholder Governance: National AI Authority under MeitY, industry and civil society representation.

4. UN & UNESCO Initiatives

The Pact complements UNESCO’s Recommendation on the Ethics of AI (2021), which prescribes:

PrincipleUNESCO Guidance
FairnessNon‑discrimination, inclusive datasets
TransparencyExplainable models, open audits
AccountabilityClear liability and redress mechanisms
PrivacyData protection by design

5. Regional Collaborations

India has also signed a parallel pact with Mauritius (Mar 2025) to leverage AI and Digital Public Infrastructure for inclusive growth, focusing on:

  • Joint AI research labs
  • Digital health & tele‑education pilots
  • Capacity building via AI for Good regional workshops

6. Strategic Takeaways for India

  • Soft Power Boost: Strengthens India’s image as a principled tech leader.
  • Diplomatic Leverage: Positions India to influence future AI treaties.
  • Domestic Signal: Reinforces commitment to ethical AI in national flagship programs.
