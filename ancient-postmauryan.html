Post-Mauryan Period (200 BCE – 300 CE)

"From invaders to patrons — the Post-Mauryan kings turned swords into stupas." — Bibliotech

Key Rulers and Dynasties

1. Indo-Greeks (Yavanas)

  • First foreign rulers in India post-Maurya
  • Introduced Hellenistic culture & portrait coins
  • Famous ruler: Menander (Milinda) — converted to Buddhism
  • Text: Milindapanho – dialogue between Menander & Buddhist monk Nagasena

2. Shakas (Scythians)

  • Came from Central Asia
  • Ruled western India — Gujarat, Malwa, parts of Rajasthan
  • Famous ruler: Rudradaman I
  • First long inscription in Sanskrit: Junagarh Rock Inscription

3. Parthians

  • Ruled briefly in northwest India
  • Famous king: Gondophernes (associated with St. Thomas's visit)

4. Kushanas

  • Central Asian tribe, originally Yuezhi
  • Kanishka was the greatest ruler
  • Started Saka Era (78 CE)
  • Patron of Buddhism — organized 4th Buddhist Council in Kashmir
  • Spread Mahayana Buddhism to Central Asia & China

Contributions of Kushanas

  • Issued gold & copper coins (bilingual – Greek & Kharosthi)
  • Built stupas at Mathura & Gandhara (Greco-Buddhist art)
  • Patronized Nagarjuna, Asvaghosha

5. Satavahanas (Andhra Dynasty)

  • First native dynasty in Deccan post-Maurya
  • Founder: Simuka | Greatest ruler: Gautamiputra Satakarni
  • Ruled Maharashtra, Andhra, Karnataka
  • Fought Shakas, revived Brahmanism
  • Used Prakrit language, Brahmi script

Satavahana Administration

  • King called "Raja" or "Maharaja"
  • Land grants to Brahmins & Buddhist monks
  • Women also issued inscriptions (e.g. Naganika)

Art and Culture

  • Gandhara School: Indo-Greek style, Greco-Roman influence, Buddha in human form
  • Mathura School: Indigenous, red sandstone, spiritual expression
  • Amaravati School: White marble, relief sculptures, Southern style

UPSC Relevance

  • Mains GS1: Cultural & political developments after Mauryas
