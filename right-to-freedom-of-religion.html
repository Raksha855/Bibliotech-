Right to Freedom of Religion (Articles 25–28)

These Articles guarantee religious freedom and ensure secularism, which is a basic feature of the Indian Constitution.

Article 25 – Freedom of Conscience and Free Profession, Practice and Propagation of Religion

  • Every person is equally entitled to freedom of conscience and the right to profess, practice, and propagate religion.
  • Subject to public order, morality, and health.
  • State can regulate or restrict religious practices in the interest of social welfare and reform.
  • Case: Rev. Stainislaus vs State of MP – Propagation ≠ Conversion by force

Article 26 – Freedom to Manage Religious Affairs

  • Freedom to establish and manage religious institutions.
  • Can own and acquire property and administer it as per law.
  • Available to religious denominations or their sections.

Article 27 – Freedom from Taxation for Promotion of Religion

  • No person shall be compelled to pay any tax for promotion or maintenance of any particular religion or religious denomination.

Article 28 – Freedom from Attending Religious Instruction

  • No religious instruction in educational institutions funded wholly by State.
  • Religious education allowed in private or partially aided institutions (with consent).

Important Keywords

  • Secularism: Positive secularism in India (equal respect for all religions)
  • Reasonable Restrictions: Public order, morality, health
