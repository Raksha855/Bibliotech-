Origin & Structure of Earth

"You can never understand life unless you understand the ground beneath your feet." — Bibliotech

Origin of the Earth

The Earth is believed to have formed around 4.6 billion years ago from a swirling cloud of gas and dust called the solar nebula.

Key Theories:

  • Big Bang Theory: Explains origin of the universe, not Earth directly
  • Nebular Hypothesis: Earth formed from solar nebula (Kant & Laplace)
  • Planetesimal Theory: Small particles collided and coalesced into larger bodies

Interior Structure of Earth

Earth has concentric layers based on physical and chemical composition, known through indirect seismic evidence.

Chemical Layers:

Layer Depth Composition
Crust 0–35 km Silicates, rocks; continental & oceanic
Mantle 35–2,900 km Silicates of magnesium and iron
Core 2,900–6,371 km Iron and Nickel (Nife core)

Mechanical (Physical) Layers:

  • Lithosphere: Crust + upper rigid mantle (tectonic plates)
  • Asthenosphere: Semi-molten upper mantle (convection currents)
  • Mesosphere: Stronger lower mantle
  • Outer Core: Liquid, generates magnetic field
  • Inner Core: Solid, high pressure

Seismic Evidence

  • Study of P-waves and S-waves reveal internal structure
  • S-waves cannot travel through liquids → confirms liquid outer core
  • Shadow zones helped identify mantle-core boundaries
